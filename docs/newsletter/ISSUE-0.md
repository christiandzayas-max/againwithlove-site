# Issue Template and Sample (Issue 0)

Follow `VOICE.md` (no em or en dashes) and `SOURCING.md` (cite or cut) for every
section.

## The template
- **Subject line:** make it tangible and specific. Name the thing they get.
  Example: "The 3-line script for 'When are you coming home?'"
- **Preview text:** one warm line that adds to the subject, does not repeat it.
- **Again,** the human opening. Two short paragraphs. A specific, real moment,
  then a warm hello.
- **On the Horizon** one or two research items, each with a plain summary, an
  evidence label, an honest "what it means for you today," and a source link.
- **This Week's Toolkit** the tangible asset. Give it, do not describe it.
- **One Thing That Helps** a single practical tip with a tiny concrete example.
- **For You, Too** support for the caregiver. Name the feeling, give one small
  permission or step.
- **From the Circle** a real, permission-based reader tip, plus the Question of
  the Week.
- **Brain Break** a light riddle or a small moment of levity.
- **Pass It On** a one-line forward ask and the referral link.
- **With love,** the sign-off and a one-line teaser for next week.
- **Footer** entity name, unsubscribe, and why they are receiving it.

## Sample: Issue 0 (mockup for internal review, not for sending)
Research items are illustrative and labeled. The reader tip is an invitation, not
a fabricated quote. The sign-off is a placeholder pending the owner's decision.

**Subject:** The 3-line script for "When are you coming home?"
**Preview:** Plus what new research really means for you, minus the hype.

### Again,
She asks it again. "When are you coming home?" Maybe for the ninth time before
lunch. And you answer again, softly, because to her it is the first time, and the
worry is real every single time.

That moment is the whole reason we are here. Welcome to Again, With Love, a weekly
note for anyone caring for someone with dementia or Alzheimer's. Every week I will
bring you one thing you can actually use, honest news about research, and a
reminder that you are not doing this alone.

### On the Horizon
*(Illustrative. In real issues the Research Radar fills this with verified,
current findings and a link to each source.)*

- **Prevention.** A review of everyday habits linked to lower dementia risk.
  **Evidence: peer-reviewed review.** What it means for you today: caring for your
  hearing, moving your body, and staying socially connected keep coming up.
  Supportive habits, not guarantees. Worth asking the doctor about.
- **Treatment.** An early report on a new drug candidate that slowed decline in a
  small group. **Evidence: preprint, not yet peer-reviewed, very early.** What it
  means for you today: interesting, but years from any clinic, and it may not pan
  out. I will keep watching so you do not have to.

I will never sell you false hope. When something is early or shaky, I will tell
you plainly.

### This Week's Toolkit
The "When are you coming home?" reassurance script. Fill in the blanks, then say
it out loud once so she can hear it from you anytime.

> "Hi Mom. It's David. I love you. I am at work, and I will see you after lunch.
> You are safe, and I am thinking about you."

Keep it short, warm, and in the present tense. If you use the Again, With Love
app, record it there so she can tap and see your face and hear your voice
whenever the worry comes back.

### One Thing That Helps
Step into her world instead of correcting it. If she asks for her mother, who
passed years ago, "Mom is gone" reopens the loss every time. Try, "Tell me about
your mom. What was she like?" You meet the feeling instead of the fact. It is
gentler for both of you.

### For You, Too
The tenth time you hear the same question, you feel a flash of irritation, and
then the guilt right behind it. Hear me on this. That does not make you a bad
daughter or a bad husband. It makes you tired and human. Your small permission
this week: take five minutes that belong only to you. Coffee on the patio. One
song, loud. A door closed for a quiet minute. You cannot pour from an empty cup.

### From the Circle
This is where a real caregiver's tip will live each week. So let me ask you: what
is the one question your loved one asks the most? Hit reply and tell me. Your
answer may help another family next week, and it helps me know what to write.

### Brain Break
A little breather. I have keys but open no locks. I have space but no room. You
can go in, but you cannot go inside. What am I? (Answer next week.)
